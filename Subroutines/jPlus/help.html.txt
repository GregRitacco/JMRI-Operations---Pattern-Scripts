<h2>j Plus Subroutine</h2>
<h3>Description</h3>
<p>The j Plus subroutine adds enhanced railroad detail to reports generated by both JMRI and this plugin.</P>
<p>Data for this subroutine is entered into the text boxes or if the o2o subroutine is activated, the additional railroad detail is imported from the TrainPlayer layout.</P>
<p>Not all fields need be entered.</P>
<p>The enhanced fields are as follows:</P>
<h4>Operating Railroad Name</h4>
<p>This is railroad that owns or operates the tracks being modeled. Any name can be entered such as 'PRR' or 'BN UPRR Joint' or 'G&D'.</P>
<h4>Operational Territory</h4>
<p>This is the railroad's name for the part being modeled, such as 'Long Beach Intermodal Terminal' or 'Salem Glass Works Branch'.</P>
<h4>Location</h4>
<p>Where the the railroad is located, such as 'The Great Divide' or 'The Bronx, New York'.</P>
<h4>Year Modeled</h4>
<p>Which year the model railroad is based on, this is a mirror of 'JMRI/OperationsPro/Settings/Year Modeled'.</p>

<h4>j Plus Subroutine settings in configFile.json</h4>
<pre>
  "jPlus" :{  <font color="blue">jPlus Subroutine settings</font>
    "OR" : "", <font color="blue">Operating railroad, the one that owns the tracks.</font>
    "TR" : "", <font color="blue">Territory. Name of the particular operation being modeled.</font>
    "LO" : "", <font color="blue">Location of the prototype.</font>
    "YR" : "", <font color="blue">Calendar year being modeled.</font>
    "SC" : "", <font color="blue">Model railroad scale, from settings or TrainPlayer.</font>
    "LN" : "", <font color="blue">Layout name, from settings or TrainPlayer.</font>
    "BD" : "", <font color="blue">Build date from TrainPlayer.</font>
  },
</pre>
